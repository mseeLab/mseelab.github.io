A PhD

What does a PhD mean?

A PhD stands for "philosophiae doctor", literally "teacher". It signals that its bearer knows a field well enough to be able to teach about it. Teaching is a ubiquitous skill: every type of job involves training others in the skills required and/or explaining the job's importance to others. Because of this, a PhD is valuable across any number of jobs beyond "just" teaching positions (though perhaps not necessary for most of them). The way that a PhD guarantees someone is able to teach is by expecting PhDs to make a new contribution to a field of knowledge. Since you must be sure that the new knowledge is actually new, you must know the field of knowledge in depth. Since nobody can teach you knowledge that doesn't yet exist, it means you taught it to yourself, so you must be able to teach (at least to yourself—most PhD programs can do a better job of teaching how to teach, though).
